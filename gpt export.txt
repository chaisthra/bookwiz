What’s the difference between couldn’t care less and couldn’t care more 

Good question — this one trips up a lot of people.

“I couldn’t care less” ✅ (correct, common)
This means:

I care so little that it’s impossible for me to care any less.

So it expresses complete indifference.

Example:

“What people think about my playlist? I couldn’t care less.”

“I couldn’t care more” ❌ (usually incorrect)
Taken literally, this means:

I care so much that it’s impossible for me to care any more.

But almost no one intends that meaning when they say it — so it usually sounds wrong or confusing.

If someone does mean deep care, they should say:

“I care a lot.”

“I care deeply.”

“I couldn’t care more about this.” (rare, but grammatically possible with context)

Why people mix it up
People often mean to say:

“I don’t care at all”

…but accidentally say:

“I couldn’t care more” (which actually means the opposite).

It’s one of those phrases where logic matters more than tone.

Easy memory trick
Less = less care = don’t care

More = more care = care deeply

So:

✅ couldn’t care less → indifference

❤️ couldn’t care more → maximum care (rare usage)

If you want, I can also explain why people say “I could care less” (the famously wrong-but-popular version 😄).
